Mohammad Sayeef Alam

Health Economist | Statistical Genetics Researcher | PhD Candidate

Hi there, welcome to my personal digital space.

I am currently pursuing research on the genetics of celiac disease as a PhD candidate in the Department of Public Health and Nursing at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology. My primary responsibilities include analysis of large-scale genetic datasets to determine novel associations, develop methods enabling precision medicine and modelling environmental and clinical risk factors.


Previously I was employed at Parexel as an Associate in the Advanced Analytics team. I was heavily invested in health economics outcome research consultancy involving but not limited to statistical methods related to economic evaluations, development of advanced excel dashboard and R-Shiny apps.


I am passionate about leveraging my statistical programming acumen to solve complex challenges in health sector requiring inter-disciplinary knowledge of medicine, health economics, computer science and statistics.

Nordic Shortfall Calculator

Developed a proof-of-concept model that calculates the absolute and proportional shortfall of 5 Nordic countries (Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden). Provides up-to-date estimates of quality-adjusted life expectancy based on EQ-5D values with comparative options between different value sets, discount rates, and dynamic inputs.

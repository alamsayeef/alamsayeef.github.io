Combined cox regularized regression method for variable selection in high dimensional microarray data

Dr Atanu Bhatatcharjee, Dr Chander Shekhar, Mohammad Sayeef Alam
April 2021

Abstract

Cancer is the second leading cause of death of which 30% to 50% can be prevented as per WHO. Cancer survival research has immensely benifitted from the advent of microarray data collection. Microarray data has tens of thousands of genes for only a handful of samples leading to its high dimensional nature. Subsequently, the requirement for variable selection becomes utmost important specially when survival outcome is concerned.

We analyzed a microarray data with gene expression values along with some clinical covariates of patients with lungs adenocarcinoma. We used a cox-regularized regression to strain parsimonious set of genes. The siginificance of the selected genes towards death was assessed using cox regression.

Both the regularized as well as the combined approaches identified 25,42 and 42 genes respectively. The brier score for the gene signature created using the selected pool from cox-lasso, cox-elastic net and combination of the two was found to be 0.1579, 0.1499 and 0.1499 respectively. Out of the combined gene pool only 19 genes were found to be significant in predicting death

The present study emphasizes the advantage of combining the two approaches for variable selection. Though in our study same brier score is observed for the combined and cox-elastic net, it proved to be better than the cox-lasso aproach. Along with clinical covariates, it shows a promising prognosis of patients. These pathways can help develop a panel of significant biomarkers for the prognosis of lungs cancer which can later be generalized for other cancers.

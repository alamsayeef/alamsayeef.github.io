Dynamics of fertility resumption behaviours of Indian women: semi-parametric survival analysis using NFHS 2015-16 cross-sectional survey

Abhijit Biswas, Dr Illias K Sheikh, Mohammad Sayeef Alam
April 2021

Abstract

Shortening of intervals between last birth and resumption of sexual behavior could result in severe health risks for mother and child. The declining duration of amenorrhea and insusceptibility and the marginal increase in abstinence all point towards adverse health outcomes. Contrarily, the contraceptive rates are also on a decline pointing towards the severity of the issue. With the dearth of information on postpartum durations and the predictors of time to end of those durations. This study focused on understanding the factors leading to the resumption of postpartum sexual contact.

The National Family Health Survey 2015-16 data was used for analysis. Data of all the women who gave birth in the last three years preceding the survey were included. Currently breastfeeding mothers were censored for postpartum amenorrhea, abstinence and insusceptibility. The Kaplan Meier Product Limit was used to estimate the survival and hazard function, while Cox Proportional Hazard Regression was used to fit the model the time-to-end of postpartum behaviors at 5% level of significance.

While the overall median duration of amenorrhea, abstinence and insusceptibility in India are 5, 3 and 7 months respectively, 73.2%, 60.5% and 49.6% women had ended respective postpartum behaviors within first 6 months. While the incidence rate varied with socio-demographic characteristics, the log rank test were indicative of significant difference in time-to-ending for those characteristics. The “hazard” for earlier resumption of sexual activity was lower among middle aged women (aHR = 0.89 95% CI: 0.79 - 0.91 for amenorrhea, aHR = 0.86 95% CI: 0.81 - 0.92 for abstinence, aHR = 0.84 95% CI: 0.79 - 0.91 for insusceptibility), it was significant higher among higher birth order women and those who were using contraception (aHR = 1.047 95% CI: 1.01 - 1.08 for amenorrhea, aHR = 1.052 95% CI: 1.02 - 1.09 for abstinence, aHR = 1.106 95% CI: 1.07 - 1.15 for insusceptibility) and (aHR = 1.351 95% CI: 1.07 - 1.7 for amenorrhea, aHR = 1.634 95% CI: 1.32 - 2.03 for abstinence, aHR = 1.645 95% CI: 1.3 - 2.08 for insusceptibility) respectively.

Higher risk for ending postpartum abstinence, amenorrhea and insusceptibility was among teenage women from central region, following hinduism, using a contraceptive method, residing in rural region with no education and higher birth order and were unemployed. Hence,policymakers and reproductive health stakeholders should design effective intervention targeted at this group of women as a means of promoting safer health for mother and child alongside controlling fertility.
